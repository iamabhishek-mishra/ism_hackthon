FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the most common questions from participants

No, participation in Miningthon 2026 is completely free of charge. There is no registration fee for any round of the hackathon.

Absolutely. Students from Computer Science, Electronics, Mechanical, Civil, Environmental Engineering, and any other branch are welcome. Miningthon values interdisciplinary teams and diverse skill sets.

No, solo participation is not allowed. Teams must have a minimum of 2 members and a maximum of 5. We encourage you to find teammates from your institution or connect with others through our community channels.

Yes, cross-institutional teams are allowed and encouraged. Members can be from different colleges, universities, or organizations as long as all members meet the eligibility criteria.

Round 1 requires a written submission of up to 2 pages including: a clear problem statement, your proposed solution approach, expected impact, and a brief implementation plan. No prototype is required at this stage.

Shortlisted teams will receive details about travel reimbursement and accommodation at ISM Dhanbad. On-campus accommodation will be arranged for outstation participants. Specific details will be communicated after shortlisting.

You are free to use any programming language, framework, hardware platform, or technology stack. Open-source libraries and APIs are permitted with proper attribution. Cloud services may be used but solutions must be demonstrable offline during the finale.

Shortlisted teams will be notified via email by March 20, 2026. Results will also be published on this website. Please ensure your registered email is active and check your spam folder.

No, theme selection is final at the time of registration. Please choose your theme carefully. If you have a genuine reason for a change, contact us at miningthon@ism.ac.in before the registration deadline.
