Overview

About Miningthon 2026

A deep dive into India's most impactful mining-focused hackathon — its mission, structure, and who should participate

What is it?

Redefining Mining Through Innovation

Miningthon 2026 is a 48-hour national-level hackathon organized jointly by the Indian School of Mines (ISM), Dhanbad, and Coal India Private Limited. It is designed to harness the power of technology and creative thinking to address the most pressing challenges facing India's coal mining sector.

Participants work in teams to develop prototypes, software solutions, hardware models, or policy frameworks that can be directly applied to real-world mining operations. The hackathon bridges the gap between academia, industry, and innovation.

With a total prize pool of ₹9,00,000 and direct industry exposure, Miningthon is the launchpad for the next generation of mining technologists.

Organized by
ISM Dhanbad

Objectives

What We Aim to Achieve

Miningthon is built around three core pillars that define the future of responsible mining

Safety in Mining

Develop technologies and systems that reduce accidents, improve hazard detection, and protect the lives of miners working in challenging underground and surface environments.

Automation & Efficiency

Leverage robotics, IoT, and AI to automate repetitive and dangerous tasks, optimize equipment utilization, and increase overall operational efficiency in coal mines.

Environmental Sustainability

Create solutions that minimize the ecological footprint of mining operations — from dust suppression and water management to land reclamation and carbon reduction.

Eligibility

Who Can Participate?

Miningthon welcomes innovators from all disciplines and backgrounds

Undergraduate Students

B.Tech / B.E. students from any recognized Indian university. Mining, CS, Mechanical, Electrical, and allied branches are all welcome.

Postgraduate & PhD

M.Tech, M.Sc., MBA, and PhD scholars with a passion for applied research and industry problem-solving in the mining domain.

Young Professionals

Working professionals under 30 years of age from the mining, energy, or technology sectors with innovative ideas to contribute.

Team Size: 2 to 5 members per team. Cross-institutional teams are encouraged. At least one member must be enrolled in or employed by an Indian institution or organization.

Structure

Three-Round Journey

From idea to implementation — a structured path to the Grand Finale

Round 1 — Online

Idea Submission

Teams submit a 2-page problem statement and proposed solution. Evaluated on innovation, feasibility, and relevance to mining challenges. Top 50 teams advance.

Round 2 — On-site

48-Hour Prototype Sprint

Shortlisted teams build and refine their solutions at ISM Dhanbad with access to labs, mentors from Coal India, and technical resources.

Round 3 — Grand Finale

Judging & Awards

Teams present prototypes to a panel of industry experts and academics. Winners announced at the closing ceremony with prizes, trophies, and certificates.
